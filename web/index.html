How ninety minutes kills a group chat

  1. /royale opens the pool.
    One tap to join. Joining mints you a Solana keypair — that key signs every pick you make from here on. Lobby locks at kickoff.
  2. Rounds fire on real events.
    Kickoff, every goal, half-time, the 75th minute, full time — each fires a sudden-death question answerable only from the live TxLINE stats feed. Sixty seconds. Wrong or silent = eliminated.
  3. The dead get funerals.
    Tombstone card, fatal pick, minute of death, eulogy from the graveside announcer. Ghosts spectate and get one /haunt. The living each carry one /jinx — the KIRICOCHO — and if their cursed rival dies that round, the chart credits the kill.
  4. One fan is left standing.
    The survivor gets a certificate: their full path of correct signed picks, hashed and anchored in Solana's Memo program. The first fan out gets the forfeit contract — the group votes a punishment, it's notarized in the same Memo trail, and it settles only with photographic /proof.

Why it can't welch

Every answer becomes sha256(pool | round | player | pick | salt), signed with the player's own ed25519 key. Each round's signed picks are merkle-rooted and written to Solana devnet before the outcome is announced. The survivor's certificate hash lands in the same Memo trail.

So: picks can't be backdated, eliminations are recomputable from the match stats, and the certificate verifies in any browser — no wallet, no RPC key, no trusting us. Try it on a real certificate.

The trust model, plainly

No money ever moves — the stakes are glory and forfeits, which is why your group chat will actually play it. Keys are custodial today (your nan doesn't have a seed phrase); the signatures and anchors are real, and the roadmap swap is client-side keys in a Mini App. Everything a judge — or your most insufferable mate — needs to re-check an elimination is public.
